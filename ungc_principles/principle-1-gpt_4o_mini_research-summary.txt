# UN Global Compact (UNGC) Principle One: Human Rights

**Core Focus**: Businesses should support and respect the protection of internationally proclaimed human rights.

## Key Requirements and Obligations

### Respecting Human Rights
- **Due Diligence**: Businesses must conduct due diligence to avoid infringing on human rights and address any adverse impacts.
- **Compliance**: Companies must comply with all applicable laws and respect internationally recognized human rights, including the **International Bill of Human Rights** and **core ILO Conventions**.
- **Contextual Awareness**: Assess the country and local context for human rights challenges, utilizing resources from NGOs, government, and international organizations.
- **Impact Assessment**: Identify whether the company is causing or contributing to adverse human rights impacts through its activities.
- **Relationships Analysis**: Evaluate relationships with governments, business partners, and suppliers to avoid complicity in human rights abuses.

### Policy Commitment
- **Public Statement**: Adopt a public policy statement approved by the board, outlining the companyâ€™s commitment to respect human rights.
- **Stakeholder Engagement**: Involve stakeholders in the development of the human rights policy.
- **Operational Integration**: Ensure the policy is reflected in operational policies and procedures.

### Human Rights Due Diligence
- **Ongoing Process**: Implement a continuous process to identify, prevent, and mitigate negative human rights impacts.
- **Stakeholder Consultation**: Engage with affected stakeholders during the due diligence process.
- **Performance Tracking**: Regularly monitor and audit human rights impacts and performance.

### Remediation
- **Grievance Mechanisms**: Establish effective grievance mechanisms for stakeholders to raise concerns regarding human rights impacts.

## Supporting Human Rights
- **Core Business Activities**: Integrate support for human rights into core business functions.
- **Social Investment**: Engage in strategic social investment and philanthropy to promote human rights.
- **Advocacy**: Participate in public policy engagement and advocacy for human rights.
- **Partnerships**: Collaborate with other organizations to advance human rights initiatives.

## Implementation Guidance
- **Training and Awareness**: Foster understanding of human rights among employees and management.
- **Integration into Business Processes**: Incorporate human rights considerations into existing business practices.
- **Communication**: Report on human rights performance and progress, ensuring transparency and accountability.

## Special Considerations
- **Vulnerable Groups**: Pay special attention to the rights of vulnerable groups, including women, children, and indigenous peoples.
- **Context-Sensitive Practices**: Adapt practices to areas with weak governance or high human rights risks.

By adhering to these principles, businesses can not only mitigate risks but also enhance their reputation, stakeholder relationships, and overall contribution to sustainable development.
