# UN Global Compact (UNGC) Principle Six: Labour

**Core Focus**: Businesses should uphold the **elimination of discrimination** in respect of employment and occupation.

## Definition of Discrimination
Discrimination in employment refers to treating individuals differently based on characteristics unrelated to their merit or job requirements. Common characteristics include:
- **Race**
- **Colour**
- **Sex**
- **Religion**
- **Political opinion**
- **National extraction**
- **Social origin**
- **Age**
- **Disability**
- **HIV/AIDS status**
- **Trade union membership**
- **Sexual orientation**

### Forms of Discrimination
- **Direct Discrimination**: Explicitly denying equal opportunity based on characteristics.
- **Indirect Discrimination**: Neutral rules or practices that lead to exclusion.
- **Cultural Discrimination**: Rooted in cultural attitudes requiring specific approaches.

## Importance of Non-Discrimination
- **Business Impact**: Discrimination leads to social tensions, limits access to a diverse talent pool, and negatively affects performance and reputation.
- **Positive Outcomes**: Embracing diversity can enhance productivity, innovation, risk management, and customer satisfaction.

## Actionable Guidance for Companies

### Compliance and Awareness
- **Respect Local Laws**: Adhere to all relevant local and national laws regarding employment.
- **Understand Diversity**: Recognize the diverse backgrounds and needs of the workforce.

### Specific Actions to Eliminate Discrimination
- **Policy Development**:
  - Institute policies that prioritize qualifications, skills, and experience for recruitment and advancement.
  - Assign high-level responsibility for equal employment issues and establish clear policies.
  
- **Evaluation and Transparency**:
  - Regularly assess job requirements to avoid systematic disadvantages.
  - Maintain transparent records on recruitment, training, and promotion.

- **Training and Awareness**:
  - Conduct **unconscious bias training** for staff.
  - Provide training on non-discrimination policies, including disability awareness.

- **Grievance Procedures**:
  - Develop procedures to address discrimination complaints and appeals.
  - Create an environment that encourages employees to raise concerns.

- **Accessibility**:
  - Adjust the physical environment to ensure health and safety for employees with disabilities.
  - Promote access to skills development and training programs.

### Community Engagement
- **Support Equal Opportunities**: Encourage community programs that foster tolerance and equal access to occupational development.
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: In foreign operations, accommodate local cultural traditions to ensure equal employment access for women and minorities.
