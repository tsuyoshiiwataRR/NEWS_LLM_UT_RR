# Principle Three: Labour

Businesses should uphold the **freedom of association** and the effective recognition of the **right to collective bargaining**.

## Core Focus
This principle emphasizes the importance of allowing both employers and workers to freely establish and join organizations that promote and defend their occupational interests, fostering a collaborative environment for negotiation and dialogue.

## Key Requirements

### Freedom of Association
- **Respect the right** of all workers to form and join a trade union of their choice without fear of intimidation or reprisal, in accordance with **national law**.
- Ensure a **climate free of violence, pressure, fear, and threats** for workers to make free decisions.
- Allow for the **formation, administration, and election** of representatives by both employers and workers.

### Collective Bargaining
- Engage in **voluntary negotiations** between employers and workers regarding terms and conditions of work.
- Recognize representative organizations for the purpose of collective bargaining.
- Adhere to the **principle of good faith**, which requires:
  - Genuine and constructive negotiations.
  - Avoidance of unjustified delays in negotiations.
  
## Implementation Guidance

### In the Workplace
- Establish **non-discriminatory policies** regarding trade union organization and membership.
- Provide workers’ representatives with **appropriate facilities** to assist in developing effective collective agreements.
- Do not interfere with the activities of worker representatives, allowing practices such as:
  - Collection of union dues on company premises.
  - Posting of trade union notices.
  - Distribution of union documents.

### At the Bargaining Table
- Use collective bargaining as a **constructive forum** for addressing working conditions and terms of employment.
- Address issues such as:
  - Restructuring and training.
  - Redundancy procedures.
  - Safety and health issues.
  - Grievance and dispute settlement procedures.
- Provide necessary **information** for meaningful bargaining.
- Balance dealings with the most representative trade union to ensure the viability of smaller organizations.

### In the Community of Operation
- Acknowledge the role of **national employers’ organizations**.
- Take steps to improve the climate in **labour-management relations**, especially in countries lacking adequate institutional and legal frameworks for recognizing trade unions and collective bargaining.

## Importance for Companies
- Establishing genuine dialogue with workers' representatives can lead to better understanding and resolution of issues.
- Promotes **constructive dialogue** that benefits enterprises, stakeholders, and society.
- Contributes to a "decent work" cycle that enhances productivity, incomes, and profits.
- Facilitates local responses to a globalized economy and supports sustainable growth and secure investment returns.
